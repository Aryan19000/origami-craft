Origami is a great way to enhance many different skills.
Finding simple and easy step-by-step instructions for origami can be quite irritating.
Here are some origami crafts put together to ease the search of them.

  • Camel
    • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
    • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
    • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
    • There are over 160 words for camel in Arabic alone.

Chameleon

  • Chameleon
    • Almost half of all known species of chameleon live in Madagascar.
    • They mainly change color in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
    • Unlike many lizards, chameleon can't regrow their tails.

Pigeon

  • Pigeon
    • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
    • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.
    • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.

Teddy-bear

  • Teddy-bear
    • A teddy bear is a stuffed toy in the form of a bear.
    • Teddy bears have a depth of character that other toys don't have, and in playing with one a child brings it to life.

Panda

  • panda
    • Pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
    • An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.
    • Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.

Cicada

  • Cicade
    • Cicadas have one of the longest insect lifespans.
    • More than 3,000 species of cicada exist.
    • They can buzz louder than a lawnmower.
    • Their wings repel water and bacteria.
